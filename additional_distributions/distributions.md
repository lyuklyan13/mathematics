## **Logistic Distribution**
The Logistic distribution visually resembles the Normal distribution but has significantly heavier tails, providing more robust modeling against outliers. It is the underlying derivative of the logistic function. In data science, it forms the probabilistic basis for Logistic Regression, used globally for binary classification tasks.


## **Negative Binomial Distribution**
While the Poisson distribution assumes the mean equals the variance, real-world data science datasets (like website clicks or customer churn counts) often exhibit variance far exceeding the mean (overdispersion). The Negative Binomial distribution solves this by acting as a mixture of Poisson distributions with Gamma-distributed rates, making it the preferred algorithm for modern count-based predictive modeling.
