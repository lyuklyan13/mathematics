## **In conclusion**

Десять простих правил ефективної статистичної практики.

1) `Statistical methods should allow the data to answer scientific questions:` Ask   yourself, “Why am I doing this?” rather than focusing on which method to use.   
2) `Signals are always accompanied by noise:` it is the attempt to distinguish between these two factors that makes an object interesting. Variability is inevitable, and probability models are useful as an abstraction.
3) `Plan ahead, really ahead:` This includes the idea of ​​prior specification in   confirmatory experiments – avoiding the researcher’s degrees of freedom   
4) `Worry about data quality:` It all depends on the data.
5) `Statistical analysis is more than a set of calculations:` don't just enter formulas or run procedures in software without knowing why you are doing it.
6) `Keep it simple:` basic communication should be as simple as possible – don’t flaunt complex modeling skills unless they are truly necessary.
7) `Provide estimates of variability:` with the caveat that the margins of error are usually larger than stated.
8) `Test your assumptions:` and clearly explain when it was impossible.
9) `When possible, copy!:` Or encourage others to do so.
10) `Make your analysis reproducible:` others should have access to your data and code.
